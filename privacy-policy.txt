## 2. What Data We Collect and Our Lawful Basis

We collect the following personal data. The lawful basis under GDPR Article 6 for each category is noted below.

- **Account data:** email address and encrypted password when you register
  *Lawful basis: Performance of a contract — necessary to create and manage your account.*

- **Event data:** event name, date, guest names/nicknames, and photos taken at your event
  *Lawful basis: Performance of a contract — necessary to deliver the Snapshot Co service you have purchased.*

- **Payment data:** processed securely by Stripe — we do not store card details
  *Lawful basis: Performance of a contract and compliance with a legal obligation (financial record-keeping under Irish tax law).*

- **Usage data:** browser type, device type, and IP address for security and diagnostics
  *Lawful basis: Legitimate interests — we have a legitimate interest in maintaining the security and performance of our service, which is not overridden by your privacy interests.*

## 3. How We Use Your Data

We use your data to:

- Provide and operate the Snapshot Co service
- Process payments via Stripe
- Store and display your event photos
- Send transactional emails (payment confirmation, album reveal notification)
- Comply with legal obligations

We do not sell your data to third parties. We do not use your data for advertising.

## 4. Photos and Guest Data

Photos taken at your event are stored securely in Supabase Storage. Access is restricted to:

- You (the event host)
- Guests you have invited, after the reveal time

Photo storage duration depends on your subscription tier. Photos are permanently deleted after album expiry unless you have an active Archive subscription.

**Your responsibility:** As the event host, you are responsible for obtaining consent from guests before they take photos.

## 5. Data Sharing

We share data only with:

- **Stripe** — payment processing (stripe.com/privacy)
- **Supabase** — database and file storage (supabase.com/privacy)
- **Vercel** — hosting (vercel.com/legal/privacy-policy)

All third-party providers are GDPR-compliant and process data under appropriate data processing agreements.

## 6. GDPR Principles

We process personal data in accordance with the seven principles set out in Article 5 of the GDPR:

1. **Lawfulness, fairness and transparency** — We process data lawfully, fairly, and in a transparent manner as described in this policy.
2. **Purpose limitation** — Data is collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes and is not processed in a manner incompatible with those purposes.
3. **Data minimisation** — We collect only data that is adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary in relation to the purposes for which it is processed.
4. **Accuracy** — We take reasonable steps to ensure that personal data is accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date.
5. **Storage limitation** — Data is kept in a form that permits identification of individuals for no longer than is necessary for the purposes for which it is processed.
6. **Integrity and confidentiality** — We process data in a manner that ensures appropriate security using technical and organisational measures against unauthorised or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage.
7. **Accountability** — As data controller, we take responsibility for and are able to demonstrate compliance with these principles.

## 7. Your Rights (GDPR)

Under GDPR, you have the right to:

- **Access** your personal data
- **Correct** inaccurate data
- **Delete** your account and associated data
- **Restrict** processing of your data
- **Data portability** — receive a copy of your data in a structured, commonly used format
- **Object** to processing based on legitimate interests

To exercise any of these rights, email: jack@eventsnapshotco.com

We will respond within 30 days.

## 8. Data Retention

- Account data: retained while your account is active, deleted within 30 days of account deletion request
- Event photos: deleted after album expiry (5, 14, or 60 days depending on tier) unless archived
- Payment records: retained for 7 years for tax compliance
